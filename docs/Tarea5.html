Exercise 6.1 Muestre que el movimiento Browniano satisface \[ E\left[\left|W\left(t\right)-W\left(s\right)\right|^{2}\right]=\left|t-s\right| \]

Si \(t>s\). \[\begin{align*} E\left[\left|W\left(t\right)-W\left(s\right)\right|^{2}\right] & =E\left[\left(W\left(t\right)-W\left(s\right)\right)^{2}\right]\\ & =t-s, \end{align*}\]

mientras que si \(t\leq s\). \[\begin{align*} E\left[\left(W\left(t\right)-W\left(s\right)\right)^{2}\right] & =E\left[\left(W\left(s\right)-W\left(t\right)\right)^{2}\right]\\ & =s-t, \end{align*}\]

por lo tanto \[ E\left[\left|W\left(t\right)-W\left(s\right)\right|^{2}\right]=\left|t-s\right| \]

Exercise 6.2 Dado \(W\left(t_{i}\right)\) y \(W\left(t_{i+1}\right)\), muestre que la variable aleatoria \[ W\left(t_{i+\frac{1}{2}}\right):=\dfrac{1}{2}\left[W\left(t_{i}\right)+W\left(t_{i+1}\right)\right]+\dfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{\Delta t}\xi,\xi\sim N\left(0,1\right) \] es un movimiento Browniano.

6.0.0.0.1 Es claro que al ser un refinamiento del movimiento browniano.

\[ W\left(0\right)=0 \]

6.0.0.0.2 \(C_{2}\). Notemos que

\[ W_{i+\frac{i}{2}}-W_{i}=\dfrac{1}{2}\left[W\left(t_{i+1}\right)-W\left(t_{i}\right)\right]+\dfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{\Delta t}\xi, \]

Sabemos que la combinación lineal de normales es una nornal. Luego, partiendo que \(t_{i+1}-t_{i}=\Delta t\). \[\begin{align*} E\left[W_{i+\frac{i}{2}}-W_{i}\right] & =0,\\ \text{Var}\left[W_{i+\frac{i}{2}}-W_{i}\right] & =\dfrac{1}{4}\Delta t+\dfrac{1}{4}\Delta t=\dfrac{1}{2}\Delta t, \end{align*}\]

Por lo tanto \(W_{i+\frac{1}{2}}-W_{i}\sim N\left(0,\dfrac{\Delta t}{2}\right)\).

6.0.0.0.3 Calculamos la esperanza.

\[ E\left[\left(W_{i+1}-W_{i+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]=E\left[\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\left[W\left(t_{i+1}\right)-W\left(t_{i}\right)\right]+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right)\dfrac{1}{2}\left[W\left(t_{j+1}\right)-W\left(t_{j}\right)\right]+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right], \]

defina \(dW_{i}=W_{i+1}-W_{i},\)

\[\begin{align*} E\left[\left(W_{i+1}-W_{i+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right] & =E\left[\left(\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right)\left(\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right)\right]\\ & =E\left[\dfrac{1}{4}dW_{i}dW_{j}+\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi+\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi+\left(\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right)^{2}\right]\\ dW_{i},dW_{j}\text{ son independientes} & =\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\right)\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}\right]\right)+E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]\\ & +E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]+\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}\left(E\left[\xi\right]\right)^{2}\\ & =\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\right)\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right]\right)+E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]+\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}\left(E\left[\xi\right]\right)^{2}\\ & =\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\right)\left(E\left[\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]\right)+E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]+\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}\left(E\left[\xi\right]\right)^{2}\\ & =\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\right)\left(E\left[\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]\right)+E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{j}+\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}\xi\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]\\ & =\left(E\left[\dfrac{1}{2}dW_{i}\right]\right)\left(E\left[\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]\right)+E\left[\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]\sqrt{\dfrac{\Delta t}{4}}E\left[\xi\right]\\ & =E\left[\left(W_{i+1}-W_{i+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right]E\left[\left(W_{j+1}-W_{j+\frac{1}{2}}\right)\right], \end{align*}\] teniendo asi, que los incrementos son independientes.

Exercise 6.3 Generalice la fórmula del ejercicio anterior para en el caso donde, \(W\left(t_{i}\right),W\left(t_{i+1}\right),\) y \(\alpha\in\left(0,1\right)\) el valor \[ W\left(t_{i}+\alpha\Delta t\right), \]

es un movimiento Browniano.

Notemos que \[ t_{i}+\alpha\Delta t+\left(1-\alpha\right)\Delta t=t_{i+1}, \] entonces vamos a definir \[\begin{align*} W_{i+\alpha} & =W\left(t_{i}+\alpha\Delta t\right)\\ & =\left(1-\alpha\right)W_{i}+\alpha W_{i+1}+Y, \end{align*}\] donde \(Y\) será una v.a independiente de \(W\left(t\right)\). Entonces \[\begin{align*} W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i} & =\left(1-\alpha\right)W_{i}+\alpha W_{i+1}+Y-W_{i}\\ & =\alpha\left(W_{i+1}-W_{i}\right)+Y\\ & =\alpha\left(W_{i+1}-W_{i}\right)+Y. \end{align*}\]

Entonces \[ E\left[W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i}\right]=E\left[Y\right], \]

por lo tanto, \(E\left[Y\right]\) tiene que ser cero. Luego \[ \text{Var}\left[W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i}\right]=\alpha^{2}\Delta t+\text{Var}\left[Y\right], \]

notemos que \[ \left(i+\alpha\right)\Delta t-i\Delta t=\alpha\Delta t, \]

por lo tanto tendría que cumplirse \(\text{Var}\left[W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i}\right]=\alpha\Delta t\).

\[ \alpha^{2}\Delta t+\text{Var}\left[Y\right]=\alpha\Delta t, \]

entonces \[ \text{Var}\left[Y\right]=\Delta t\left(\alpha-\alpha^{2}\right), \]

como \(Y=\sqrt{\alpha\left(1-\alpha\right)}\xi,\xi\sim N\left(0,1\right)\). Como este es un refinamiento del browniano, entonces se cumple \(C1\). \[ W\left(0\right)=0. \]

Conseguimos \(C2\) por construcción y de forma análoga tenemos la independiencia de los incrementos. \[ E\left[\left(W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i}\right)\left(W_{j+\alpha}-W_{j}\right)\right]=E\left[W_{i+\alpha}-W_{i}\right]E\left[W_{j+\alpha}-W_{j}\right]. \]

Exercise 6.4 Suponga que \(X\sim N\left(0,1\right)\). Sabemos que \(E\left[X\right]=0\) y \(E\left[X^{2}\right]=3\). Luego, de la definición, el \(p-\) ésimo momento satisface \[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x. \]

Usando la relación, muestre que \(E\left[X^{3}\right]=0\) y \(E\left[X^{4}\right]=1\).

Entonces deduzca el incremento Browniano, \[ \Delta W_{i}=W\left(t_{i+1}\right)-W\left(t_{i}\right), \]

satisface \(E\left[\Delta W_{i}^{3}\right]=0,E\left[\Delta W_{i}^{4}\right]=3\left(\Delta t\right)^{2}\). Entonces encuentre una expresión para \(E\left[X^{p}\right]\) para un entero positivo \(p\).\ Pista: Tu puedes usar el dato que \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\exp\left(-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\right)=\sqrt{2\pi}.\)

Considere la fórmula. \[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x. \]

Partiendo la integral, \[ \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x=\underbrace{\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{0}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x}_{I_{1}}+\underbrace{\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x}_{I_{2}} \]

Entonces \[ I_{1}=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{0}x^{p}\exp\left(-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x, \]

hagamos el cambio de variable. \(y=-x,\) tenemos que \[\begin{align*} I_{1} & =\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{\infty}^{0}-\left(-y\right)^{p}\exp\left(-\dfrac{y^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}y\\ & =\dfrac{\left(-1\right)^{p}}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}y^{p}\exp\left(-\dfrac{y^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}y=I_{2}\left(-1\right)^{p}, \end{align*}\] entonces \[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\left(1+\left(-1\right)^{p}\right)I_{2}, \]

de aqui tenemos, que si \(p\) es impar \(E\left[X^{p}\right]=0,\) entonces si \(p\) es par \[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=2I_{2}, \]

entonces, nos concentraremos en \[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{p}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)\mathrm{D}x,p=2k,k\in\mathbb{N} \]

Considere \(y=\dfrac{x^{2}}{2},\mathrm{D}y=x\mathrm{D}x\).

\[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{p-1}\exp\left(-y\right)\mathrm{D}y, \]

luego \(\sqrt{2y}=x,\) entonces \[\begin{align*} E\left[X^{p}\right] & =\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}\left(\sqrt{2y}\right)^{p-1}\exp\left(-y\right)\mathrm{D}y\\ & =\dfrac{2\left(\sqrt{2}\right)^{p-1}}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}y^{\frac{p-1}{2}}\exp\left(-y\right)\mathrm{D}y\\ & =\dfrac{2\left(\sqrt{2}\right)^{p-1}}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}y^{\frac{p+1}{2}-1}\exp\left(-y\right)\mathrm{D}y, \end{align*}\] recordando la función Gamma. \[ \Gamma\left(z\right)=\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{z-1}e^{-t}\mathrm{D}t, \]

entonces

\[ E\left[X^{p}\right]=\begin{cases} 0 & p\text{ impar}\\ \dfrac{2^{\frac{p+1}{2}}}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\Gamma\left(\dfrac{p+1}{2}\right) & p\text{ par} \end{cases}, \]

entonces \(E\left[X^{4}\right]=\dfrac{4}{\sqrt{\pi}}\Gamma\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right)\)

\[\begin{align*} \Gamma\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right) & =\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{\frac{3}{2}}e^{-x}\mathrm{D}x. \end{align*}\]

Considere \[\begin{align*} u=x^{3/2} & \mathrm{D}v=e^{-x}\mathrm{D}x\\ \mathrm{D}u=\dfrac{3}{2}x^{1/2} & v=-e^{-x}, \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \Gamma\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right) & =\left[-x^{3/2}e^{-x}\right]+\dfrac{3}{2}\int_{0}^{\infty}x^{1/2}e^{-x}\mathrm{D}x\\ & =\dfrac{3}{2}\dfrac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}. \end{align*}\]

Entonces \[ E\left[X^{4}\right]=3 \]

Notemos que si \(\Delta W\sim N\left(0,\sigma^{2}\right)\), entonces \[ Z=\dfrac{\Delta W}{\sigma}\sim N\left(0,1\right) \]

En general, \[ E\left[\left(\Delta W\right)^{p}\right]=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}w^{p}\exp\left(-\left(\dfrac{w}{\sigma}\right)^{2}\right)\mathrm{D}w, \]

considere \(\sigma u=w\), entonces \[\begin{align*} E\left[\left(\Delta W\right)^{p}\right] & =\sigma^{p}\left[\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}u^{p}\exp\left(-u^{2}\right)\mathrm{D}w\right],\\ & =\sigma^{p}E\left[Z^{p}\right], \end{align*}\]

entonces para \(p=4,\sigma^{2}=\Delta t\). \[ E\left[\left(\Delta W\right)^{4}\right]=\left(\Delta t\right)^{2}E\left[Z^{4}\right]=3\left(\Delta t\right)^{2} \]

Exercise 6.5 Suponga que \(X\sim N\left(0,1\right)\). Muestre que para \(a,b\in\mathbb{R}\), \[ E\left[\exp\left(a+bX\right)\right]=\exp\left(a+\dfrac{1}{2}b^{2}\right). \] Deduzca que \[ E\left[\exp\left(t+\dfrac{1}{4}W_{t}\right)\right]=\exp\left(\dfrac{33}{32}t\right) \]

Considere \[ E\left[\exp\left(a+bX\right)\right]=e^{a}E\left[\exp\left(bX\right)\right], \]

notemos que \(bX\sim N\left(0,b^{2}\right),\) por lo tanto, la función generadora de momentos nos dice que \[ E\left[\exp\left(bX\right)\right]=M_{bX}\left(1\right)=\exp\left(\dfrac{b^{2}}{2}\right), \]

por lo tanto \[ E\left[\exp\left(a+bX\right)\right]=e^{a}\exp\left(\dfrac{b^{2}}{2}\right)=\exp\left(a+\dfrac{1}{2}b^{2}\right), \]

ahora, considere \[ E\left[\exp\left(t+\dfrac{1}{4}W_{t}\right)\right]=E\left[\exp\left(t+\dfrac{1}{4}\left(W_{t}-W_{0}\right)\right)\right], \]

Notemos que \(W_{t}-W_{0}\sim N\left(0,t\right),\) por lo tanto, usando la fórmula anterior

\[\begin{align*} E\left[\exp\left(t+\dfrac{1}{4}\left(W_{t}-W_{0}\right)\right)\right] & =\exp\left(t+\dfrac{1}{4}\left(\sqrt{t}X\right)\right),X\sim N\left(0,1\right)\\ & =\exp\left(t+\left(\dfrac{1}{4}\sqrt{t}\right)X\right)=\exp\left(t+\dfrac{t}{32}\right)\\ & =\exp\left(\dfrac{33}{32}t\right) \end{align*}\]
