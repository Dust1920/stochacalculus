3  Tarea 2

Exercise 3.1 Sea \(X_{i}\) v.a.i.i.d tales que

\[ \mathcal{P}\left[X_{i}=h\right]=\mathcal{P}\left[X_{i}=-h\right]=\dfrac{1}{2},\forall i, \]

entonces definimos \(Y_{n,h}\).

Queremos calcular la función característica de \(Y_{n,\delta}\).

\[ E\left[i\lambda Y_{n,\delta}\left(t\right)\right], \]

Aprovechando que para cada \(X_{i}\) son v.a.i.i.d. Entonces, tenemos lo siguiente

\[\begin{align*} E\left[i\lambda Y_{n,\delta}\left(t\right)\right] & =\left(\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right)^{t/\delta}\\ & =u^{t}, \end{align*}\]

donde \[\begin{align*} u & =\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]^{1/\delta}\\ \ln\left(u\right) & =\dfrac{1}{\delta}\ln\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right] \end{align*}\]

Entonces, aproximaremos \(\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\) con su expansión de Taylor. \[ \cos\left(\lambda h\right)\approx1-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}, \]

entonces \[\begin{align*} \ln\left(\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right) & \approx\ln\left[1-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}\right]\\ & \approx-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!} \end{align*}\]

Entonces \[\begin{align*} u^{t} & \approx\exp\left[\dfrac{t}{\delta}\left(-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}\right)\right],\\ & \approx\exp\left[-\dfrac{t}{\delta}\left(\dfrac{\lambda^{2}h^{2}}{2}-\dfrac{\lambda^{4}h^{4}}{24}\right)\right], \end{align*}\]

Calculando el limite \[ \lim_{\delta\to0}E\left[\exp\left(i\lambda Y_{n,\delta}\left(t\right)\right)\right]=\lim_{\delta\to0}\exp\left[-t\left(\left[\dfrac{h^{2}}{\delta}\right]\left(\dfrac{\lambda^{2}}{2}-\dfrac{\lambda^{4}h^{2}}{24}\right)\right)\right], \]

si \(h^{2}/\delta\to\infty\) Segun la sucesión \(\delta_{n}\to0\) tenemos limites diferentes, por lo tanto, este no existe. Ahora, usando la normalización, retomando las operaciones anteriores,

\[\begin{align*} E\left[\exp\left(i\lambda Y_{n,\delta}\left(t\right)+\dfrac{th^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right)\right] & =E\left[\exp\left(i\lambda\sum_{i=0}^{n}X_{i}+\dfrac{th^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2\delta}\right)\right]\\ & =E\left[\exp\left(i\lambda\sum_{i=0}^{n}X_{i}\right)\right]\exp\left(\dfrac{th^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2\delta}\right)\\ & =\left(\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]^{1/\delta}\exp\left(\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2\delta}\right)\right)^{t}, \end{align*}\]

entonces, \[\begin{align*} v & =\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]^{1/\delta}\exp\left(\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right)\\ \ln v & =\ln\left[\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]^{1/\delta}\exp\left(\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right)\right]\\ & =\dfrac{1}{\delta}\ln\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]+\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2\delta}\\ & =\dfrac{1}{\delta}\left(\ln\left[\cos\left(\lambda h\right)\right]+\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right)\\ & \approx\dfrac{1}{\delta}\left(\ln\left[1\underbrace{-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}}_{x_{0}}\right]+\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right) \end{align*}\]

recordando que \[ \ln\left(1+x\right)\approx x-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}, \]

entonces

\[ \ln v\approx\dfrac{1}{\delta}\left(\left[-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}-\dfrac{\left(-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{4!}\right)^{2}}{2}\right]+\dfrac{h^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right), \]

bajo la simplificación de que \(o\left(h^{k}\right)\equiv0,k\geq4,\) entonces \[\begin{align*} \ln v & \approx\dfrac{1}{\delta}\left(\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{24}-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{8}\right)\\ & \approx\dfrac{1}{\delta}\left(\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{24}-\dfrac{3\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{24}\right)\\ v & \approx\exp\left(-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{12\delta}\right) \end{align*}\]

por lo tanto, si \(h^{4}/\delta\to0\) \[ \lim_{\delta\to0}E\left[\exp\left(i\lambda Y_{n,\delta}\left(t\right)+\dfrac{th^{2}\lambda^{2}}{2}\right)\right]=\lim_{\delta\to0}\exp\left(-\dfrac{\left(\lambda h\right)^{4}}{12\delta}\right)=1 \]
